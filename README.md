# The 7: A Body-Based Mental Health Framework

A dimensional, person-first alternative to categorical psychiatric diagnosis (DSM/ICD).

## The Problem

Current psychiatric classification doesn't work:
- **Same diagnosis, different people**: Two people with "depression" can share almost zero symptoms
- **Arbitrary comorbidity**: 50-70% have 2+ disorders because boundaries are fake
- **Treatment roulette**: 30% respond to first medication because diagnosis doesn't predict what helps
- **Identity capture**: "You ARE depressed" instead of "your HEART is currently low"

## The Alternative

Instead of asking "which bucket do you fit in?", ask:

**Where are you on each dimension, and where do you want to be?**

## The 7 Characteristics

| Char | Question | Spectrum |
|------|----------|----------|
| **HEAD** | Where's your attention? | scattered ←→ locked |
| **GUARD** | How alert is your protection? | down ←→ up |
| **HEART** | Can you feel alive? | numb ←→ chasing |
| **GUT** | How much fuel? | empty ←→ overflowing |
| **GRIP** | How tightly holding on? | loose ←→ tight |
| **GROUND** | Are you here? | floating ←→ frozen |
| **REACH** | How do you connect? | pulled in ←→ merged |

Each maps to where people actually feel it in their body.

## Key Principles

1. **No position is inherently "right"** — context determines whether a position serves you
2. **Both ends can be problematic** — not "more is better" or "less is better"
3. **States, not traits** — this is where you ARE, not who you ARE
4. **You define the target** — not a norm, not a professional, you
5. **Change is visible** — track movement on each dimension over time

5. **Track change**: Monitor movement over time
