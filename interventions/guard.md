## Moving GUARD from UP toward TUNED

*For: hypervigilance, chronic anxiety, panic, always on edge*

### Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

**Moves**: GUARD from up toward tuned
**Type**: somatic
**Evidence**: research
**Source**: Ma et al. 2017, "The Effect of Diaphragmatic Breathing on Attention, Negative Affect and Stress"

**What it is**:
Inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Repeat for 4+ minutes.

**How it works**:
Activates parasympathetic nervous system via vagal tone. Slows heart rate, reduces cortisol.

**Notes**:
Works best for acute spikes. Less effective for chronic baseline elevation without consistent practice.

---

### Cold Water Face Immersion (Dive Reflex)

**Moves**: GUARD from up toward tuned
**Type**: somatic
**Evidence**: research
**Source**: Porges, Polyvagal Theory; various dive reflex studies

**What it is**:
Immerse face in cold water for 30 seconds, or apply cold pack to face while holding breath.

**How it works**:
Triggers mammalian dive reflex, activating vagus nerve and rapidly lowering heart rate.

**Notes**:
Fast-acting for acute panic. Not a long-term solution.
