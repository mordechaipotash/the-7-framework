## Moving GROUND from FLOATING toward ROOTED

*For: dissociation, depersonalization, "who am I?", not in body*

### 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

**Moves**: GROUND from floating toward rooted
**Type**: somatic/cognitive
**Evidence**: clinical
**Source**: Standard trauma therapy technique

**What it is**:
Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can hear, 3 you can touch, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste.

**How it works**:
Forces sensory engagement with present environment. Interrupts dissociative drift by anchoring to immediate reality.

**Notes**:
Most effective for acute dissociation. For chronic disconnection, more intensive approaches needed.

---

### Feet on Floor

**Moves**: GROUND from floating toward rooted
**Type**: somatic
**Evidence**: clinical/anecdotal
**Source**: Somatic therapy traditions

**What it is**:
Press feet firmly into floor. Feel the contact. Notice the support. Stand if possible.

**How it works**:
Brings attention to base of body. Physical contact with ground as metaphor becomes literal.

**Notes**:
Can be done anywhere, anytime. Simple but surprisingly effective for mild floating states.
